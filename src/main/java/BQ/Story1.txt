
Optimize "ViewBy VM/BS" in VAMOS PA EU Expansion

1. Situation

I worked on the Vamos system, a core business system that was first built and launched in the JP region.
We then launched a global expansion initiative, and my team was in charge of supporting the Vamos EU expansion as part of the PA program.
My team served as the host team for the underlying service framework and platform, while the EU team acted as the business feature owner,
responsible for implementing business logic on top of our platform. After launching the same view by VM/BS logic in the EU region,
we encountered a critical issue: the feature worked stably in JP, but in the EU, the API kept timing out consistently and could not return valid responses to users.
Initially, we were unsure of the root cause.

2. Task

I needed to quickly identify the root cause of the API timeouts, restore service availability urgently,
and design a long-term architecture-level solution to support the Vamos EU expansion smoothly.

3. Action

First, I conducted root cause identification: I analyzed online logs, monitoring metrics, and distributed tracing data to troubleshoot the issue.
I found that the number of VM/BS dimensions in the EU scenario was far larger than that in JP, which led to an extremely high volume of downstream RPC calls
and heavy network I/O, exhausting thread pool resources and causing persistent timeouts.

Then, I implemented a short-term fix: To avoid blocking the EU launch, we quickly applied a temporary solution by increasing the thread pool size
to prevent resource exhaustion, ensuring the API could respond within the required time.

Next, I designed and implemented a long-term optimization: As the framework host team, we took strong ownership. Immediately after the business launch,
we promoted a fundamental solution. We redesigned the logic: instead of sending separate downstream requests for each view-by dimension,
we fetched all detailed data in one single RPC call and performed aggregation locally in memory.
This transformed massive network I/O operations into lightweight in-memory computing.

Finally, I promoted implementation and knowledge sharing: We aligned the solution with the EU team, rolled out the change,
and verified its stability through testing and online observation. After the project, we documented the entire problem, root cause, and solution in detail,
and shared our findings and lessons learned in a group meeting to help other teams avoid similar issues.

4. Result

- Reduced downstream RPC calls from dozens or hundreds to just one, significantly reducing network I/O pressure.

- Eliminated API timeouts completely and significantly improved service latency, ensuring stable service operation.

- Successfully supported the Vamos EU expansion, enabling stable business operation in the EU region.

- Improved the efficiency and scalability of the underlying framework, making this optimization pattern reusable for other regional expansions.

- Shared best practices across the team, preventing similar performance issues in future projects.
